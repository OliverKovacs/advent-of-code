# Math

$$
\begin{aligned}
s(x) &= x * (t - x) > d \\
s(x) &= -x^2 + tx > d \\
s(x) &= x^2 - tx \le -d \\
s(x) &= x^2 - tx + d \le 0 \\
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
x_{1,2} &= -\frac p 2 \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac p 2\right)^2 - q} \\
p &= -t \\
q &= d \\
x_{1,2} &= \frac t 2 \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac {-t} 2\right)^2 - d} \\
\end{aligned}
$$
